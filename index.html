About

Assessment, done carefully.

Psychological assessment impacts critical decisions in healthcare, education, and public policy. Although these decisions depend on the classification of individuals (i.e., placing individuals in categories), the methods we use for classification often face challenges related to fairness, accuracy, and interpretability. My research aims to address these challenges by integrating machine learning approaches with psychometric theory to improve classification accuracy. As a quantitative psychologist, my research centers on two interconnected lines of work: (1) methodological research, focused on developing and evaluating tools for classification and variable selection under complex psychometric conditions such as differential item functioning (DIF) or missing data; and (2) applied psychometrics and classification research.

I have extensive experience with item response theory, factor analysis, and supervised machine learning for classification and variable selection, along with large simulation studies of how classifiers behave under difficult conditions such as unmodeled differential item functioning and missing data.
