A methods lab for trustworthy classification. We build and stress-test the psychometric and machine learning tools that decide who gets measured, and how well.

Mission

Accurate, fair, interpretable.

Psychological classification is only as good as the measurement underneath it. When standard assumptions break, through item bias, missing data, or unmodeled complexity, ordinary methods quietly lose accuracy, and the people most likely to be misclassified are often those already underserved. CARAT studies where those failures happen and which methods hold up, pairing item response theory and factor analysis with machine learning, and testing everything through large simulation studies before recommending it for real data. Much of our applied work centers on misophonia, where reliable measurement is still being built.

The name

Five commitments, one acronym.

C

Classification

Building and comparing classifiers for psychological data, and asking when a machine learning model beats a traditional psychometric one.

A

Assessment

Evaluating and sharpening measurement instruments, from full-length scales to efficient short forms that keep their precision.

R

Replication

Asking whether findings hold up, including whether factor structures themselves replicate across samples and studies.

A

Algorithms

Random forests, regularized regression, and genetic algorithms for classification and variable selection under realistic conditions.

T

Testing

Monte Carlo simulation as the proving ground: stress-testing methods under item bias, missing data, and multidimensionality before they reach applied work.

Current themes

What we are working on

Classification & item bias

Diagnostic accuracy under unmodeled DIF

Comparing IRT and random forest classification when differential item functioning cannot be directly modeled, now extending to multidimensional assessments.

Algorithms & missing data

Variable selection when data are incomplete

Benchmarking LASSO, elastic net, and genetic-algorithm selection across missing-data mechanisms, with reproducible tutorials in R.

Assessment & misophonia

Measuring and characterizing misophonia

Psychometric evaluation of the Duke Misophonia Questionnaire, a short form, and a latent profile analysis of symptom subtypes.
